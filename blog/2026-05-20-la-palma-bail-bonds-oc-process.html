The La Palma Scenario

La Palma is a small North OC city that does not operate its own PD — Cypress PD provides contract police services. A defendant arrested in La Palma gets booked at Cypress PD's jail, then typically transferred to the Anaheim Detention Facility within 12-24 hours.

La Palma-Specific Facts

La Palma's status as a contract-city for police services creates a booking pipeline that surprises families. Cypress PD operates the holding facility at 5275 Orange Ave; the Anaheim Detention Facility is the regional transfer point for North OC contract cities (La Palma, Cypress, Stanton, Buena Park unincorporated areas). Knowing where the defendant is at each stage determines where to file the bond.

Two-stage filing strategy: file the bond at the receiving facility (Anaheim) rather than the initial booking PD (Cypress). The bond routes correctly to wherever the defendant is currently housed, but filing at the destination skips administrative overhead during the transfer window. Most North OC contract-city cases process this way.

North OC cases arraign at the West Justice Center in Westminster. Judges there tend to apply schedule bail on first appearance with downward modification on defense motion under In re Humphrey (2021) — the Humphrey precedent requires courts to consider ability-to-pay before imposing cash bail. Defense counsel typically files the bail review motion within 48 hours of arraignment if schedule bail is unaffordable.

The OC bail schedule for La Palma misdemeanors typically runs $1,000-$25,000; felonies $25,000-$100,000. Bond cost at 10% under California Insurance Code §1800.4: $100-$10,000 depending on charge. La Palma residents often have access to family/friend co-signers (indemnitors) given the affluent North OC demographic — bond approval rates run higher here than in lower-income areas where indemnitor qualification is harder.

What PC §853.6 Actually Says

Penal Code §853.6: Release on cite or bail — booking officer authority. Understanding the statute matters because bail, no-bail rules, and protective orders all flow from charging decisions.

Bail Cost and the 10% Premium

The 10% statutory premium under California Insurance Code §1800.4 is a fee for the surety service, not money lent. Non-refundable regardless of case outcome. No licensed bondsman can lawfully charge a different rate.

Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Don't wait for arraignment — bail can post once booking is complete.
  2. Don't pay cash bail without checking with a bondsman — ties up full amount for 6-18 months.
  3. Don't sign indemnitor paperwork unread — it's a binding contract.
  4. Don't coach the defendant on jail calls — they're recorded.
  5. Don't violate any protective order, even verbally — PC §166 stacks fast.

Is PC §853.6 bondable?

Most PC §853.6 cases are bondable. Exceptions: no-bail holds, parole holds, immigration detainers.

Can the protective order be modified?

Yes — protected party requests modification at arraignment or later hearing.

What if case is dismissed?

Must I use a bondsman?

No. Cash bail option exists but ties up full amount.
