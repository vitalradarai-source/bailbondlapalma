How Domestic Violence Bail Works in Orange County

When a person is booked at Theo Lacy, a bail amount is assigned based on the charge. Orange County operates on a published bail schedule, so the amount is determined by the specific Penal Code section, not by a judge at that initial stage. A bail bondsman can post bail directly against that schedule amount without waiting for an arraignment hearing. Arraignment for La Palma cases is held at the North Justice Center, 1275 N Berkeley Ave, Fullerton, CA 92832 — typically within 48 hours of arrest on business days. Getting your loved one out before that first court date means they can meet with an attorney, gather evidence, and walk into arraignment prepared rather than sitting in a jail cell.

One important legal point that often confuses families: an Emergency Protective Order (EPO) is almost always issued when La Palma PD responds to a domestic disturbance. An EPO restricts contact between the parties — it does not block bail from being posted. These are separate legal mechanisms. The person arrested can be released on bail while the EPO remains in effect; they simply cannot contact the protected party until the order is modified or expires.

What You Pay with a Bail Bond

Under California Insurance Code § 1800.4, the premium for a bail bond is fixed at 10% of the total bail amount. This rate is set by state law — no licensed bail agent in California can legally charge more or less. For a $50,000 bail on a PC § 273.5 felony, the bond premium is $5,000. For a $20,000 misdemeanor bail, it is $2,000. That premium is the fee for the bail agent's guarantee and is not returned after the case concludes, but it is far less than posting the full cash bail amount yourself. Frequently Asked Questions

No. An EPO limits contact between the accused and the protected party — it does not block the bail process. The two are legally independent of each other.
